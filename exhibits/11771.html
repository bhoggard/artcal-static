Summer Projects

Marian Spore
55 33rd Street, Fourth Floor, 646-620-7758
Brooklyn Misc.
July 31 - December 31, 2010
Reception: Friday, July 30, 8 - 10 PM
Web Site

On 30 July, new works will be added to the Marian Spore collection.

Thom Kubli’s FLOAT! Thinktank 21 is an experience that takes place in a egg-shaped floatation tank. One at a time, visitors float in the tank and listen to a five-minute audio work by the Berlin-based sound artist, and then float in silence for forty minutes. To book a session in the tank, please email [email protected]

Christopher Robbins has consulted with residents of Sunset Park about repairs needed in their neighborhood. Under the auspices of his project WPA2010, he will hire workers from the neighborhood to fill a pothole outside 36th Street Station, thereby creating a unique object in the Marian Spore collection.

Joe Winter will reveal a new installation of his ongoing project at Marian Spore, ‘Models for a History of Light.’ For this work, Winter creates beautiful abstract photograms using ordinary construction paper exposed to light over long periods of time.

The opening will be followed by an afterparty from 10PM to 1AM

Marian Spore is an accumulative museum of contemporary art in Industry City, Brooklyn. New works are periodically added to a steadily growing exhibition in a 16,000 square foot space.
