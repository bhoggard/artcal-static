Eung Ho Park: Endless

Y Gallery (Bowery Location)
355A Bowery, (basement), 917 721 4539 / 212 228 2880
East Village / Lower East Side
November 3 - November 21, 2010
Reception: Wednesday, November 3, 7 - 10 PM
Web Site

Y Gallery is pleased to present Endless, a solo exhibition by Korean artist Eung Ho Park. Park bases his work in observations of social relationships and personal experiences as an immigrant. He transforms groupings of mundane objects, whether spoons, bottle caps or bowling balls, into installations that depict contemporary narratives of humanity.

Eung Ho Park was born in Woonchun, South Korea and he received his BFA from Pratt Institute. Eung Ho Park has exhibited at Exit Art, the Drawing Center in New York City, Sculpture Center, the Brooklyn Museum of Art, the Queens Museum of Art, the Long Island University, Wake Forest University in South Carolina among others. His work has been reviewed in The New York Times, The Los Angeles Times, The Baltimore Sun, Art on Paper, The Boston Globe, and other publications. Eung Ho Park currently has a solo exhibition at The Jamaica Center for Arts on view until January 2011. He lives and works in New York.
