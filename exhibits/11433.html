Escape from by Natalia Porter

Eyelevel BQE
364 Leonard Street, at Withers, 917-660-4650
Williamburg
May 15 - June 20, 2010
Reception: Saturday, May 15, 5:30 - 9 PM
Web Site

Beyond “Stairway to Heaven”, “Jacob’s Ladder”, and even “Step by Step” the conception of accession is deeply embedded in the human psyche, as are the ways in which we ascend, or descend for that matter. Escape From is Natalia Porter’s first solo show at Eye Level BQE, and the site-specific installation presents a succession of staircases that diverge in scale and materials.

In Escape From, the ladders, steps, and staircases invoke both architectural function, and metaphoric symbolism. Traditionally stairs, ladders, and accession represent advancement, attainment, and accomplishment. However, the accumulation of many many miniature stairs and ladders provoke feelings of repetition, notions of time, structure and system, as well as to the every day passage of life. Natalia’s work engages existing architectural elements within the gallery, shifting the viewer’s perception of the space, and implying a narrative that co-mingles the history of the gallery with that of the artist.

Come experience this detailed and rich show yourself at the opening May 15th, or throughout the course of its exposition through June 10th at EyeLevel BQE gallery. Preview images, and additional information on the artist available at eyelevelgallery.com. This show is proud to be generously supported by the Mexican Cultural Institute.
