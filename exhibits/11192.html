Spring Exhibition with Lucinda Luvaas, Andy Sovia and Randolph SanMillan

Skylight Gallery NYC
538 West 29th Street, Second floor, 212-629-3131 x248
Chelsea
April 8 - May 16, 2010
Reception: Thursday, April 8, 6 - 9 PM
Web Site

Skylight Gallery presents a Spring Exhibition with Lucinda Luvaas, Andy Sovia and Randolph SanMillan.

Andy Sovia paints dreamscapes with a surrealist twist. He has had numerous solo shows as well as many group exhibitions including The Brooklyn Museum. Andy has been painting since he was thirteen years old.

Lucinda Luvaas has developed a unique technique of painting that she calls “imprinting,” which are relief works on wood panels of very intricate scenes. Lucinda makes her home in Southern California where she works full time on her fine and video art. In addition to the Skylight Gallery her work is represented by The James Gray Gallery, Bergamot Station, Santa Monica and Studio West Gallery, OR.

Randolph SanMillan originally hales from Haiti and his passion is to create sculpture that is unique and provoking. Emotional, sensual, and spatial energies mingle to capture the essence of a concept in unusual ways. In this show Randolph will display bronze and wood sculptures.
