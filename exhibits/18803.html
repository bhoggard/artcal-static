Interwoven December 14, 2012 – March 3, 2013 A Group Exhibition and Grand Opening celebration of Warren Lewis Sotheby’s International Realty Williamsburg

November 2012, Brooklyn, NY – Warren Lewis Sotheby’s International Realty in collaboration with Causey Contemporary Gallery is pleased to present Interwoven, a group exhibition of gallery artists. The opening reception of this salon show will also mark the grand opening of Warren Lewis Sotheby’s International Realty office in their new Williamsburg, Brooklyn location.

Interwoven will unite many different styles, voices and processes representative of the gallery. The exhibition will feature works by Bahar Behbahani, Nina Carelli, Lauren Dreier, Elise Freda, Kathy Goodell, Howard Gross, Melissa Murray, Norman Mooney, and Carri Skoczek. Interwoven will bring abstraction to mingle with representation; it will allow minimalism to converse with effusive detail; and ultimately it will present the emergence of similarities and differences between these diverse artists.

Bahar Behbahani is an Iranian artist who lives and works in both New York and Tehran. Ms. Behbahani has had her paintings, video art and installations featured in numerous locations throughout Europe and Asia, as well as in major cities throughout the U.S. Nina Carelli is originally from Martha’s Vineyard, MA, and currently lives and works in Brooklyn, NY. Her etchings have been featured in solo and group exhibitions in the NYC area. She is also the founder and co-editor of a self-published art magazine and collective, Creeps Annual. Lauren Dreier is a cross-discipline artist and designer based in Philadelphia. With a background in architecture and material studies, Lauren has taught design courses at The University of Pennsylvania, while exhibiting her work in venues around Philadelphia and New York City. Elise Freda has been developing and exhibiting her paintings for over 30 years. Her works have appeared in both group and solo shows throughout the Eastern United States, as well as in major publications and collections. Kathy Goodell is a contemporary artist who works in sculptural objects, installation and drawing. For over 30 years she has exhibited her work in galleries both nationally and internationally, has been featured in several notable art books, and has taught in art institutions across the country. Howard Gross began his artistic career in Chicago after attending the Art Institute, and later relocated to New York City in the mid-70’s. His sculpture, drawings and conceptual installations have been shown in many esteemed galleries in New York City amongst other cities. Melissa Murray is an emerging artist based in Bushwick, Brooklyn. Her surreal drawings have been featured in solo and group exhibitions in galleries throughout the U.S. and also in Canada. Norman Mooney was born in Dublin, Ireland and has been based in Brooklyn for nearly 20 years. His metaphysical drawings and sculptural works have been shown in galleries and museums in the U.S. and abroad, including 4 solo exhibitions in New York City in 2012. Carri Skoczek is a Brooklyn-based painter, printmaker, costume designer and voodoo doll maker. She has exhibited extensively for the last 23 years across the country.

Interwoven will be on view at the Warren Lewis Sotheby’s International Realty office through March 2nd, 2013. The public is invited to celebrate the opening of Interwoven, as well as the grand opening of Warren Lewis Sotheby’s International Realty on December 14th, 2012 from 6-9 pm at their Williamsburg office, 299 Bedford Avenue, Brooklyn NY 11211. This event has been made possible by a generous sponsorship from Citi Bank.
