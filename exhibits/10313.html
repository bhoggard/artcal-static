New Works By Keyholder Artists In Residence

Lower East Side Printshop
306 West 37th Street, 6th Floor, 212-673-5390
Hell's Kitchen
November 3, 2009 - January 17, 2010
Reception: Friday, November 20, 6 - 9 PM
Web Site

Saul Becker, Rosalia Bermudez, Eyal Danieli, Sara Eichner, Caleb Freese, Tai Hwa Goh, Viviane Rombaldi Seppey, and Matthew Trygve Tung

Lower East Side Printshop is pleased to announce NEW WORK BY KEYHOLDER ARTISTS IN RESIDENCE, on view at the Printshop from November 3, 2009 – January 17, 2010. This presentation will feature new works on paper created through the Printshop’s Keyholder Residency. The Keyholder Residency offers emerging artists free access for one year to professional printmaking facilities, providing them the opportunity to develop new work and experiment with printmaking as a medium.

OPEN STUDIO Golnar Adili, Dan Alvarado, Rachel Beach, Eyal Danieli, Hope Dector, Caleb Freese, Tai Hwa Goh, Annica Lydenberg, Cristina Moroño, and Liz Zanis

Friday November 20th, 2009 Hours: 6 – 9pm FREE and OPEN to the PUBLIC

To complement the exhibition, the Printshop will host an OPEN STUDIO, opening the Artists’ Studio to the public and showcasing current work being created in the Printshop. Current studio artists Golnar Adili, Dan Alvarado, Rachel Beach, Eyal Danieli, Hope Dector, Caleb Freese, Tai Hwa Goh, Annica Lydenberg, Cristina Moroño, and Liz Zanis will be on hand to discuss their work and give insight into printmaking production.

The Lower East Side Printshop’s programs have been supported in part by the Milton and Sally Avery Arts Foundation, Carnegie Corporation of New York, Con Edison Company of New York, The Greenwall Foundation, International Fine Print Dealers Association, The Jerome Foundation, Wolf Kahn and Emily Mason Foundation, National Endowment for the Arts, Pollock-Krasner Foundation, The Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts.

This exhibition is made possible with public funds from the New York State Council on the Arts, a state agency.

This exhibition is supported, in part, by public funds from the City of New York Department of Cultural Affairs.
