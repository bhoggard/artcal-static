The 1st Five Years: NYU in Ghana

NYU The Commons Gallery
34 Stuyvesant Street, Steinhardt School of Education
East Village / Lower East Side
February 17 - February 27, 2010
Reception: Wednesday, February 17, 5 - 7 PM
Web Site

Curated by Katy Hamer and Lyle Ashton Harris

This exhibit will encompass the artistic resonance of NYU’s Study Abroad program that has existed in Accra, Ghana for the past five years. Both Steinhardt and Tisch offer courses and this exhibition will feature painting, drawing, installation, and photography. There will also be a special screening of films from Tisch students of Awam Ampka, Academic Director of NYU Ghana and NYU Africa House. This opportunity enables students to live in Ghana, take classes at NYU Facilities and other local Universities, as well as travel both throughout Ghana and to neighboring African countries.

I have collaborated with Lyle Ashton Harris in selecting a group of students who will participate in the exhibit showing work that was either created during his/her experience in Ghana or art that has been made since the experience.

I hope you will join us in revisiting this rich land through the eyes and experiences of others translated into a visual, universal language; art.

For more information please visit: http://www.nyu.edu/studyabroad/ghana/
